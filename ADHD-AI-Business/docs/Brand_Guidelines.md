# Brand Guidelines

## Brand Voice
Calm, practical, encouraging, and realistic.

## Tone
Speak like a supportive coach who understands that parents are tired and trying their best.

## Style Rules
- Use plain language.
- Give practical steps.
- Avoid clinical jargon unless clearly explained.
- Avoid fear-based hooks.
- Avoid making parents feel guilty.
- Never shame children for ADHD symptoms.

## Example Voice
Instead of:
"Your child is being defiant."

Say:
"Your child may be struggling to start, shift attention, or manage frustration."

## Disclaimer Pattern
For medical, medication, therapy, or diagnosis-related topics:
"This is educational and not medical advice. For diagnosis, treatment, or medication questions, speak with a qualified healthcare professional."
